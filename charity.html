About me

Charity work

Kirklees Summer Play Camp

As a founding member of the charity and having volunteered with them since 2013, this childrens summer camp is very close to my heart. We provide 6 fun-filled, 4-day camps for yorkshire children, aged 9-11, each summer. For a lot of the children who attend, they are from disadvantaged background and this may be their summer holiday, therefore we are determined to make it the mst fun 4 days of their summers.

Each camp involves sleeping in tents, running games and activites, and feeding all of the children. The camps are run back-to-back and I always dedicate as much time as I can while they are running. As a volunteer, I sleep in a tent for the best part of 5 weeks, if my other commitments allow. A lot of people are shocked that anyone would choose to do this but it is so much fun and extremely fulfilling.

Future Frontiers

Throughout my time at school and university, I never knew what I wanted to do for a career and I never felt like I was supported aptly enough to figure it out. While I was a student I found out about Future Frontiers, whose ethos is the stop this and guide school students in a tangible way. To give them ambition and therefore more drive to apply themselves more towards their qualifications.

I volunteered as a Lead Coach, meaning I was guiding a whole cohort of 20ish coaches who each lead 2 students through the course. Following 2 cohorts from the first session to the graduation was extremely uplifting and I learnt a lot myself. A few of the sessions were dedicated to matching the pupils with a professional an industry that they are potentially interested in and setting up a video call. This enabled them to understand the job roles available in that sector, what is required for them and they can ask any questions they have. I learnt a lot from these sessions and really saw how valuable it can be to give young people the opportunity to discuss their future with professionals from all industries.

Toybox Nursery

As part of my Duke of Edinburgh Gold Award, I volunteered for 4 hours a week, on a Wednesday afternoon, for a year at the Toybox Nursery in Wakefield. As a teenager I was quite inexperienced at working with toddlers but I soon found it easy to play and understand as well as doing the menial cleaning and other tasks that the workers were less keen on carrying out. I was completing my A levels at the time, so my Wednesday afternoons became a chance for me to switch off from my academics and do something completely different.

It was quite intimidating at times, as toddlers can be very unpredictable but I learnt to improvise with them and throughout the year my confidence grew. I was really sad to leave at the end of the year, but it was at this point that I was moving cities and going to university.
